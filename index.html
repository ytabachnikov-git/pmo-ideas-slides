🌧️ First Co-Hosted Games

Milan hosted ice events while Cortina d'Ampezzo and surrounding mountain venues hosted snow sports, spanning Lombardy, Veneto, and Trentino.

⛰️ New Sports Debut

Ski mountaineering made its Olympic debut along with new events: dual moguls, women's large hill ski jumping, and more.

🏒 NHL Returns

NHL players competed in the Olympics for the first time since 2014, bringing the world's best hockey talent back to the Games.

February 6, 2026

"Armonia" — The Opening Ceremony

A spectacular celebration at the iconic San Siro Stadium in Milan.

Venue

Stadio San Siro, Milan — Italy's largest stadium, built in 1926. Nearly 61,000 spectators witnessed the spectacle, with ticket prices up to $2,300.

Performers

Mariah Carey, Laura Pausini, and Andrea Bocelli headlined the ceremony, alongside 1,200 volunteer performers in over 1,400 costumes.

Historic Firsts

Two Olympic cauldrons were lit for the first time — one in Milan by Deborah Compagnoni & Alberto Tomba, and one in Cortina by Sofia Goggia. First Games under IOC President Kirsty Coventry.

Design Inspiration

The cauldron design was inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's geometric "knot" patterns, a tribute to Italy's artistic heritage.

Historic Moments

The stories that made Milano Cortina 2026 one for the ages.

🏒

Miracle on Italian Ice

Team USA defeated Canada in overtime for men's hockey gold — the first since the "Miracle on Ice" at Lake Placid 1980. Connor Hellebuyck made 41 saves before Jack Hughes buried the winner.

🏆

Norway Shatters Records

Norway won 41 medals, surpassing their own record of 39 set at PyeongChang 2018. Their 18 golds also broke the single-Games record of 16 from Beijing 2022.

Brazil Makes History

Brazil won their first-ever Winter Olympic medal — and it was gold. The first tropical, Latin American, and South American nation to medal at the Winter Games.

Olympic Debut

Ski Mountaineering — A New Olympic Sport

The fast-paced alpine discipline made its thrilling debut in Bormio with sprint and relay events.

⛰️ What is Ski Mountaineering?

Athletes race up and down mountain terrain, switching between skiing, carrying skis on their backs, and climbing steps. Sprint events feature elimination rounds leading to a dramatic final.

🏅 Three Medal Events

Men's Sprint, Women's Sprint, and Mixed Relay were contested at the Stelvio Ski Centre in Bormio on February 19 and 21.

🌎 Dominant Nations

France, Spain, and Switzerland proved to be the powerhouses, collecting multiple medals. The U.S. team finished 4th in the mixed relay — ahead of host Italy.

Iconic Venues

Events spanned 3 regions — Lombardy, Veneto, and Trentino — from urban arenas to alpine peaks.

🏟San Siro Stadium
Milan

Italy's largest stadium (est. 1926). Hosted the Opening Ceremony "Armonia" before 61,000 spectators.

⛳Milano Ice Arena
Milan

Hosted all figure skating and short track speed skating events in the heart of the city.

🏒Santa Giulia Arena
Milan • New Build

A brand new 12,000-seat arena designed by David Chipperfield for ice hockey.

🛳Cortina Sliding Centre
Cortina d'Ampezzo

Rebuilt version of the historic 1956 Olympic sliding track. Hosted bobsled, luge, and skeleton.

🎿Livigno Snow Park
Livigno

Near the Swiss border at high altitude. Hosted freestyle skiing and snowboarding events.
